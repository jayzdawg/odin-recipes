Ayurvedic Carrot Soup Recipe

A beautiful and pranic Carrot and Ginger Soup

Savor the warmth and health benefits of our Carrot and Ginger Soup, a perfect blend of sweet carrots and zesty ginger, rooted in Ayurvedic principles. This soup is designed to balance the doshas, particularly soothing for Vata and Kapha, while its vibrant ingredients enhance digestion, boost immunity, and promote detoxification. A comforting, nourishing bowl that's both easy to digest and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 5-6 carrots (peeled & chopped)
  • 1 1/2 cups split red lentils
  • 3 cups boiling water
  • 3 cloves garlic (peeled & chopped)
  • 2 brown onions (diced)
  • 1 inch fresh ginger
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil (or ghee)
  • 1/2 tsp. cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp. whole cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 cup veggie stock
  • 1/2 tin coconut milk
  • Handful of fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1 juiced lime

Steps

  1. In a large pot, saut√© chopped onion, garlic and fresh ginger in coconut oil for 5 minutes
  2. Add the chopped carrots and continue to cook with the lid on the pot for 10 minutes or until the carrots start to soften
  3. Then, add the split red lentils, cumin, turmeric and stock cube and stir until combined
  4. Pour in boiling water and leave to cook for a further 10-15 minutes, or until the lentils are completely broken down
  5. Add coconut milk and lime juice, and blend with a stick blender until smooth
  6. Serve with handful of cilantro on top and a piece of crusty sourdough bread on the side!
